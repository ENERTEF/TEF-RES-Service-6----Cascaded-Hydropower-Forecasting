# Wind Power — Short-Term Forecasting Service

**Author:** Public Power Corporation
**Version:** 1.0
**Last updated:** 3 Mar 2026

---

## 1. Business Context & Definitions

The Wind Power Short-Term Forecasting Service delivers day-ahead and intra-day probabilistic forecasts of wind power production at turbine and wind-farm level. Forecasts are generated by a LightGBM ensemble model that ingests outputs from five Numerical Weather Prediction (NWP) models evaluated as meteorological input candidates, together with real-time SCADA telemetry collected from each wind turbine.

The service is designed to support operational and market-facing decision-making by wind park operators, grid operators, retailers, aggregators and virtual power plant (VPP) operators. Accurate and reliable short-term forecasts are critical for energy scheduling, balancing and risk management. They enable stakeholders to reduce imbalance costs, optimise flexibility dispatch, anticipate grid constraints and maintain network reliability under varying atmospheric and operational conditions. The service is intended for continuous operational use, with defined availability, accuracy and auditability requirements.

### Key Terms

- **Wind Turbine (WT):** An electromechanical device that converts kinetic wind energy into electrical energy. Each turbine is identified by a unique `turbine_id` in the SCADA dataset.
- **Wind Farm / Wind Park:** A collection of wind turbines located at a defined geographic site, sharing common grid connection and SCADA infrastructure.
- **SCADA Data:** Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition telemetry recorded at turbine level, covering meteorological measurements, mechanical state, electrical output and operational counters.
- **NWP Model:** Numerical Weather Prediction model that produces gridded meteorological forecasts by numerically integrating atmospheric equations, or via trained ML surrogates thereof. Five weather forecasting models are evaluated: MetOffice_GlobalHiRes, DWD_ICON-EU, ECMWF_HRES, GraphCast by Google DeepMind and NCEP_GFS.
- **LightGBM:** Light Gradient Boosting Machine — a high-performance gradient-boosted decision-tree algorithm used as the core predictive model for power output forecasting.
- **Forecast horizon:** The future time window for which power predictions are generated (e.g. day-ahead: 24 hours, intra-day: 1–6 hours).
- **Temporal Resolution:** The time step at which forecasts are provided (hourly, aligned to the SCADA sampling interval).
- **Power Curve:** The functional relationship between wind speed and turbine power output, characterising turbine performance across operational wind speed ranges.
- **active_power (Wh):** Cumulative energy counter (Wh) — a monotonically increasing register. Instantaneous power output (kW) is derived from its hourly delta.
- **energy_kwh:** Hourly energy production (kWh) computed from the `active_power` delta over each hour.
- **power_kw:** Instantaneous power output (kW) — the primary forecast target variable.
- **Pitch Angle:** Blade pitch angle (degrees) — the rotational position of turbine blades relative to the wind direction, used to regulate power output.
- **Backtesting:** Rolling evaluation over historical time ranges to emulate real-time forecasting conditions for model validation.
- **Imbalance:** The deviation between scheduled and actual generation in the energy market, attracting financial penalties.

### 1.1 PPC (Park Owner Context)

This service is intended for Public Power Corporation (PPC), the owner and operator of a portfolio of wind parks in Greece, to strengthen operational planning and portfolio oversight by providing reliable short-term and day-ahead production forecasts with quantified uncertainty. PPC's primary objectives span four interrelated dimensions. First, reducing imbalance exposure by minimising deviations between scheduled and actual generation in day-ahead and intraday energy markets. Second, optimising reserve and flexibility dispatch by improving coordination between wind generation and complementary flexible assets. Third, supporting grid stability by providing accurate generation estimates to the Transmission System Operator (TSO) for balancing operations. Fourth, improving operational resilience by anticipating wind ramp events, curtailment periods and periods of elevated forecast uncertainty.

In day-to-day operations, PPC relies on forecast outputs to set production expectations for the next 24–48 hours, manage operational buffers and decide when to revise market bids and operational plans. Persistent differences between forecast and measured production are treated as diagnostic signals that can indicate performance degradation, availability limitations or emerging equipment issues.

## 2. Problem Statement

The objective is to deliver a reliable, production-grade forecasting service that predicts hourly wind power production at turbine and wind-farm level over horizons of 1–84 hours ahead. The service must operate continuously and provide forecasts that are consistent, traceable and suitable for operational use across varying atmospheric conditions and turbine operating states.

The service shall expose an authenticated interface for requesting forecasts per turbine or farm and time range, returning time-aligned outputs at hourly temporal resolution. Forecasts must be generated with a clear issue time, include sufficient metadata to support auditability and reproducibility and provide uncertainty quantification through prediction intervals.

The forecasting approach addresses three interconnected challenges:

- **NWP Model Selection:** Five weather forecasting models (MetOffice_GlobalHiRes, DWD_ICON-EU, ECMWF_HRES, Google DeepMind GraphCast, NCEP_GFS) provide wind speed and direction forecasts at the turbine locations. The service systematically evaluates and selects the best-performing NWP model(s) as inputs for each horizon, with the possibility of dynamically weighting multiple models.
- **Non-linear Power Curve Modelling:** The mapping from wind conditions to power output is non-linear, affected by turbine-specific parameters (pitch angle, rotor RPM, cut-in/cut-out speeds) and site effects (wind shear, turbulence, wake interference). LightGBM is used to capture these complex dependencies.
- **Operational State Integration:** SCADA telemetry provides real-time information about turbine operating conditions (curtailment, maintenance, performance degradation) that must be incorporated to avoid forecasting full production during unavailability periods.

The service will be assessed as an operational capability, with emphasis on forecast quality, stability over time, service reliability (availability and response performance) and NWP model comparison results.

### 3.2 NWP Weather Forecast Inputs

Five ML-based Numerical Weather Prediction (NWP) models are evaluated as input sources for meteorological variables. Each model provides gridded forecasts that are bilinearly interpolated to the GPS coordinates of each turbine. The five candidate models and their key characteristics are:

- **ML-MetOffice_GlobalHiRes (UK Met Office):** global coverage at ~10 km spatial resolution, hourly temporal resolution, updated twice daily (00/12 UTC).
- **DWD_ICON-EU (Deutscher Wetterdienst):** ~2.8 km horizontal resolution over Europe, hourly temporal resolution, updated four times daily.
- **ECMWF_HRES (ECMWF):** ~9 km spatial resolution (~0.1°), hourly temporal resolution, updated twice daily (00/12 UTC).
- **Google DeepMind GraphCast:** AI-based medium-range forecasting model applying deep learning to historical weather data.
- **NCEP_GFS (NOAA / NCEP):** ~13 km (0.125°) spatial resolution, hourly temporal resolution over 0–120 h, updated four times daily (00/06/12/18 UTC).

For each NWP model and turbine location, the following meteorological variables are extracted and aligned to the SCADA hourly time grid: wind speed at 100 m height (m/s, interpolated to hub height where applicable), wind direction at 100 m height (degrees), ambient temperature at 2 m (°C), wind gust speed (m/s) and boundary layer height (m) as a proxy for atmospheric stability.

Turbine GPS coordinates (latitude, longitude) are provided by PPC for each `turbine_id` and stored in a secure configuration registry. They are treated as static reference data and used to perform spatial interpolation from the NWP model grids.

### 3.3 Data Quality & Pre-processing

The following data quality and pre-processing steps are applied before model training and inference:

- **Timestamp alignment:** All SCADA and NWP streams are aligned to UTC hourly timestamps. Gaps shorter than 2 hours are forward-filled; longer gaps are flagged and excluded from training.
- **Outlier filtering:** `power_kw` values exceeding rated capacity by more than 5% or below zero during non-curtailment periods are removed.
- **Curtailment and maintenance flags:** Hours with `power_reference` significantly below `possible_power` are flagged as curtailed and excluded from normal forecast evaluation.
- **NWP bias correction:** For each NWP model, a rolling 30-day quantile mapping is applied to align wind speed distributions to SCADA measurements.
- **Feature engineering:** Calendar features (hour, month, day_of_week), lag features (wind speed at t−1, t−2, t−3) and 12-hour rolling statistics are computed.

## 4. Analytics, Scope & Update Frequency

- **Temporal scope:** The service provides hourly power forecasts (P10, P50, P90) at turbine and wind-farm level. Day-ahead mode covers H+1 to H+24; extended mode covers H+25 to H+84; intra-day rolling mode covers H+1 to H+6, updated every hour.
- **Update frequency:** Forecasts are generated on a scheduled cadence (once per hour, after NWP ingestion and SCADA refresh) and also on demand via authenticated API requests.
- **Output format:** For each turbine (or farm aggregate), the service returns a structured forecast package including:
  - **Issue time & validity window:** UTC timestamp when the forecast is generated, plus the start/end times the forecast covers (e.g. next 24–48 h).
  - **Time-indexed turbine forecast:** Hourly time series of P10, P50 and P90 power output (kW) and energy production (kWh) for each turbine and, optionally, farm-level aggregates.
  - **NWP input summary:** Identification of which NWP model(s) were used as primary meteorological inputs for this issue time.
  - **Traceability metadata:** Forecast ID, model version, training data cutoff date and NWP retrieval timestamp.

## 5. Evaluation Protocols & Metrics

The purpose of the evaluation is to verify that the service operates reliably and delivers consistent, accurate and operationally usable wind power production forecasts at turbine and farm level. Evaluation focuses on compliance with agreed accuracy metrics, NWP model comparison outcomes, service availability and delivery of specified forecast outputs for each request.

### 5.1 Data Usage & Forecasting Protocol

- The service shall generate forecasts on demand or on a scheduled cadence, for horizons up to 48 hours ahead of the stated issue time (UTC), aligned to hourly temporal resolution.
- Only information available at or before the issue time may be used: SCADA telemetry up to that time and NWP forecasts with issuance time at or before the issue time.
- Forecast outputs shall be time-aligned and include a unique identifier and minimal traceability fields (forecast ID, model version, NWP source).
- The service shall behave consistently under repeated requests for the same issue time and configuration.

### 5.2 Backtesting Protocol

Before training the production LightGBM model, all five weather forecasting models are evaluated over a historical backtesting period using 2021–2022 data for training and 2023 data for evaluation. Backtesting replays the evaluation protocol in strict chronological order, ensuring that at each evaluation step only information available at forecast issue time is used, thereby faithfully emulating real operational conditions and avoiding any look-ahead bias. The procedure operates as a rolling-origin evaluation:

- **Origin step:** The evaluation window advances one day at a time. At each origin t, the NWP forecast issued at or before t is retrieved for each of the five models and the corresponding SCADA measurements for the following 1–48 hours are used as ground truth.
- **Strict causality:** No future SCADA observations or NWP outputs beyond issue time t are permitted as inputs, mirroring operational deployment.
- **Horizon stratification:** Metrics are computed separately for each forecast horizon H ∈ {1, 2, …, 48} hours, enabling identification of models that excel at short (H ≤ 6 h), medium (H = 7–24 h) and extended (H = 25–48 h) ranges.
- **Seasonal stratification:** Results are disaggregated by season (DJF, MAM, JJA, SON) and wind speed regime (low: < 5 m/s; medium: 5–12 m/s; high: > 12 m/s) to detect regime-dependent model behaviour.
- **Turbine-level granularity:** Backtesting is performed independently per `turbine_id`, since local topography, wake effects and prevailing wind patterns cause significant inter-turbine variation in NWP model skill.

Based on backtesting results, a dynamic ensemble weighting strategy is applied: all five model forecasts are combined using a least-squares optimisation that minimises squared error between the weighted combination of model outputs and observed wind power generation. The resulting weights are adaptive, reflecting each model's recent predictive performance and are revisited at each monthly retraining cycle using the most recent 90 days of backtesting data.

### 5.3 Data Gaps and Exceptions

- Time periods with missing, invalid or flagged measurements in actual production shall be excluded from forecast-quality scoring.
- Hours flagged as curtailed (`power_reference` significantly below `possible_power`) shall be scored separately against a curtailment-adjusted target.
- If a turbine has insufficient recent SCADA data to produce a forecast, the service shall return a clear, structured error and such events shall be counted under service reliability KPIs.

### 5.4 Service Evaluation Metrics & KPIs

The service shall be evaluated using the following quantitative metrics and performance targets:

- **MAE (kW):** Mean Absolute Error of forecasted `power_kw` versus measured values, aggregated across turbines and horizons. Target: below 8% of rated capacity.
- **RMSE (kW):** Root Mean Squared Error of forecasted `power_kw` versus measured values, aggregated across turbines and horizons. Target: below 12% of rated capacity.
- **NMAE (%):** Normalised MAE expressed as a percentage of installed capacity, enabling scale-independent comparison across turbines of different rated power. Target: below 12%.
- **MAPE (%):** Mean Absolute Percentage Error of forecasted `power_kw` versus measured values. Reported per model and for the ensemble as a comparative diagnostic metric.
- **Skill Score vs. Persistence:** Relative improvement in RMSE compared to a naive persistence forecast (last observed `power_kw` carried forward), computed per forecast horizon. Target: above 20% improvement for H ≥ 3.
- **NWP Ranking Score:** Per-turbine RMSE ranking of each of the five NWP models across forecast horizons and seasons, reported as a comparative summary in the deliverable reports.
- **Uncertainty Calibration:** Verification that the P10–P90 prediction intervals contain approximately 80% of observed values. Target: empirical coverage between 78% and 82%.
- **Horizon-Specific Accuracy:** MAE and RMSE calculated separately for each individual forecast horizon H+1 through H+48, reported per turbine and in aggregate to characterise forecast degradation with lead time.
- **Availability:** Percentage of scheduled and on-demand forecast requests that return a valid response within the agreed service level. Target: above 99.0%.
- **Latency (P95):** 95th percentile end-to-end API response time for a single-turbine forecast request. Target: below 10 seconds.

## 6. Deliverables & Submissions

The selected provider shall deliver three reports, aligned with the lifecycle of the service, together with the required technical specifications and deployment artefacts.

### 6.1 Deliverable Reports

1. **Pre-Service Deliverable – Service Design & Setup Report**
   Submitted prior to service start, this report shall describe the proposed machine learning architecture, NWP model evaluation framework and selection rationale, LightGBM configuration and training procedure, baseline definition, data requirements and quality criteria, system architecture, security measures and integration plan. It shall also define the operational schedule and procedures for service execution.

2. **Intermediate Deliverable – Interim Performance & Operations Report**
   Submitted at an agreed midpoint of the service period, this report shall summarise service operation to date, data coverage per turbine, preliminary NWP model ranking results, accuracy metrics against baseline and any adaptations to the modelling methodology. Any issues, refinements or model retraining events shall be documented.

3. **Final Deliverable – Final Evaluation & Recommendations Report**
   Submitted at the end of the service period, this report shall present final accuracy and reliability results at turbine and farm level, a definitive NWP model comparison and selection recommendation, uncertainty calibration analysis and guidance for future service continuation, scaling or model improvements.

### 6.2 Technical Specifications & Submissions

- **Service Interface Documentation:** Full documentation of APIs, data formats, authentication and access controls.
- **Deployment Artefacts:** The provider shall specify the deployment approach. Where containerisation is used, a Dockerfile or equivalent container specification shall be delivered to support reproducible deployment. Alternative deployment models shall be clearly documented.
- **Configuration, Versioning & Handover:** Documentation of configuration parameters, model and data versioning and operational handover procedures.
- **Security & Data Protection Documentation:** Description of data handling, access control and compliance with applicable data protection requirements.
